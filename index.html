Plain answers

Questions people ask us

General information only—not medical or legal advice.

How much does rehab in Thailand cost?

Prices vary widely by provider, room, medical needs and length of stay. Many private centres quote only after an assessment. Ask for a written, itemised total—including detox, medication, psychiatric care, transfers and possible extensions—before paying.

Is rehab in Thailand safe?

Safety depends on the individual centre and your clinical needs. Verify current Thai licensing, clinician credentials, overnight medical coverage, detox protocols and the emergency hospital pathway. Do not stop alcohol, benzodiazepines or other substances suddenly without medical advice.

How long should I stay?

Many programmes begin around 28 days, but there is no universal ideal. A clinician should recommend length based on withdrawal risk, addiction history, mental health, home environment and progress—not a standard sales package.

Can my family be involved?

Often, yes. Ask whether family therapy is live or online, who facilitates it, when it begins and how the centre handles confidentiality and consent.

Will my insurance cover treatment abroad?

Some international policies may reimburse parts of treatment, but coverage differs. Get pre-authorisation directly from your insurer and ask the centre for diagnostic codes, invoices and clinical documents before admission.

Do I need a visa for treatment in Thailand?

Visa rules depend on nationality, length of stay and current Thai requirements. Confirm with an official Thai embassy or immigration source; do not rely solely on a treatment provider for immigration advice.
